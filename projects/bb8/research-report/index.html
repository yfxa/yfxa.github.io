BB-8 Build Comparison

Our BB-8 vs. the BB-8 Builders Club baseline running Joe's Drive MK3. Same mechanical bones, very different brain.

What we keep, what we change

We keep the entire Joe's Drive MK3 Flywheel mechanical stack. Motor carriage, A-frames, flywheel tray, weighted flywheel, head control arm, dome-tilt servo geometry, 12 mm hardened axle rod, slip rings, Suaoki battery pack. Zero mechanical redesign. The Joe's Drive community has burned thousands of hours validating this drivetrain and we have nothing to gain by reinventing it for a 9-week deadline.

We replace the brain and the sensing. Arduino Mega → ESP32-S3 for the real-time motor loop, with a Jetson Orin Nano Super sitting on top doing perception and planning. MPU6050 → BNO055 because on-chip fusion is cleaner. Bourns 6630 dome-spin pot → AS5600 magnetic encoder because it's $5 instead of $65-with-freight and kills our single longest-lead part. RFM69 RC remote → nothing, because the whole point of this build is that BB-8 drives itself.

Net delta over baseline: ~$590 more in parts, ~4 months less build time (because the H2D eliminates the panel paint/sand/clear-coat cycle that eats half of a typical Joe's Drive build), and every AI capability the canonical build doesn't have — camera, face recognition, gesture recognition, obstacle avoidance, autonomous navigation, delivery.
